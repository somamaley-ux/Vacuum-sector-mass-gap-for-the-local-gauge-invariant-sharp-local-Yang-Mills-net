\begin{abstract}
The measurement problem is usually framed as a problem about outcome definiteness, state reduction,
or probability. This paper treats it instead as a fixed-domain problem of reference to measurement
records. Work throughout on a downstream regime in which admissibility is already forced, standing
is bivalent on the evaluated fragment, same-scope admissibility classification is unique, the admissible
interior is unique up to lawful equivalence, and standing is conserved. Within that regime, define
admissible reference assignments for diachronic measurement-record discourse and prove a
measurement-reference quotient theorem: any admissibility-preserving presentation of the
standing-bearing content of such discourse factors uniquely through a quotient of admissible
assignments by standing-equivalence. Differences that do not survive that quotient are semantic skin;
differences that do survive it are standing-relevant.

The paper then proves an Everettian non-selection theorem for the bare unitary, entangling,
decohering package; an equivalence-collapse theorem showing that any same-scope content quotient
that preserves standing-bearing use collapses to the measurement-reference quotient; a confirmation
criterion stating that theory-level confirmation is well-defined only when lawful redescription preserves
that quotient; a fixation-commutation hinge theorem showing that without admissible-redescription
commutation theory-level confirmation is undefined rather than merely weakened; a toy witness of
hypothesis-order reversal under divergent admissible assignments; a diachronic inheritance theorem for
memory, anticipation, deliberation, and confirmation; and an exhaustion theorem stating that
interpretation packages can locate the work of singular-reference fixing at exactly one of four
structural loci:
dynamic selection, ontic privileging, semantic restriction, or discourse deflation.

The paper does not derive the Born rule or alter the Hilbert-space formalism. Its narrower claim is
that, on the target explanatory practice, the measurement problem is first a problem of referential
determinacy for measurement records and only derivatively a problem of physical outcome production.
\end{abstract}

\begin{claimbox}
\textbf{Main claim.} Measurement records are evidential anchors, not inert marks. On the fixed
downstream substrate, admissible reference assignments for those records determine a unique
standing-bearing quotient space. Bare Everettian unitary dynamics, entanglement, decoherence, and
branch-local record stability do not by themselves collapse the admissible class to one quotient class
for diachronic measurement discourse. If such collapse is not supplied elsewhere, theory-level
confirmation, memory, anticipation, and deliberation become assignment-relative rather than
theory-relative.
\end{claimbox}
